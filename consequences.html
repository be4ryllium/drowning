Consequences of Drowning

Drowning has many negative effects, such as:

  • You can develop brain damage and other long-term disabilities.
  • You can go unconscious.
  • You can go into cardiac arrest.
  • You can get Apnea and Hypoxemia.
  • You can die.

            On top of negative physical consequences, someone who nearly drowns may experience psychological harm such as being afraid of swimming for a long time. People who knew someone who drowned will have to deal with the grief of losing someone and may also develop a fear of swimming because of it.
