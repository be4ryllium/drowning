Consequences of Drowning

  • Drowning injuries can cause brain damage, long term disability, and other long term effects.
  • Drowning can cause you to go unconcious for a short amount of time.
  • Apnea, cardiac arrest, and hypoexmia are all possible side effects of drowning.
  • Certain factors affect drowning, such as not being able to swim, no supervision, the location, using alcohol and drugs, and not wearing life jackets.
  • Low and Middle Class income people make up 90% of drowning deaths worldwide.
  • In the United States of America, 45% of drowning deaths are among the most economically active segment of the population.
  • Covering wells, using doorway barriers and playpens, fencing swimming pools and otherwise controlling access to water hazards greatly reduces water hazard exposure and risk.
  • Childcare and teaching children swimming in schools is an approach that can reduce the risk of drowning amongst kids.
  • By investing in prevention, more than US$ 400 billion in potential economic losses due to drowning can be avoided. (WHO's words)
