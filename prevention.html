How to Prevent Drowning

As with most accidental methods of injury, drowning can be prevented with preemptive and active measures.

  • Being taught how to swim by parents or school can drastically decrease the chance of drowning.
  • Fencing and guarding swimming pools can decrease the risk of someone falling in.
  • Looking out for your family and friends when they are in the water is very important to make sure that they don't start drowning.
  • Lifeguards established around a body of water can look out for potential drownings.
  • Not panicking or hyperventilating is important because you lose energy less quickly.
  • If you are not a very good swimmer, sticking to the shallow end of the pool will ensure a safer experience.
  • Learning CPR may save the life of someone who is drowning.
